Messier 57 - The Ring Nebula

Messier 57, also known as the Ring Nebula, is a planetary nebula located in the constellation Lyra.

This nebula is approximately 2,300 light-years away from Earth and is a popular target for amateur astronomers.

The Ring Nebula represents a late stage in the evolution of a star similar to our Sun.

Its distinctive shape is created by the stellar winds from the dying star at its center, which has shed its outer layers.

The nebula's vibrant colors come from ionized gases, primarily hydrogen and oxygen, glowing in response to radiation from the central star.
